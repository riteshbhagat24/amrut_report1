Section 05What the Data Is Telling Us

Key Insights

Five observations that should drive every decision in the next rollout cycle.

What's Working

  • Portfolio diversification. Running five product categories in parallel has built a meaningful content library — a major asset for retargeting and cross-sell.
  • Core market density. Maharashtra, Goa, and Karnataka act as a stable test bed — insights here are statistically reliable and can be extrapolated to expansion states.
  • The Pama Malam Pan-India reach campaign — running since September — has already identified the low-CPM states. That learning should now be used to cherry-pick, not broadcast.
  • Traffic-layer activation on 11 Nov across five campaigns shows the team has recognized the need to move beyond pure awareness — that's the right instinct.

What Needs Rethinking

  • No structured funnel. Awareness, Traffic, and Lead-Gen are running as parallel silos rather than a sequenced journey. Audiences warmed up on Awareness aren't being retargeted on Conversion.
  • Too many "Unknown" placements. A meaningful share of impressions is landing in unclassified regions — likely targeting broadness that needs tightening.
  • Lozenges is MH-only. Given the product's strong seasonal relevance (cough/cold monsoon + winter), this is a clear under-deployment.
  • WhatsApp Lead-Gen ran for 6 days only. The channel was validated then shut off — it should be a permanent always-on layer, not a one-time test.
  • No regional language creative layering visible in campaign naming — which suggests the same creative is running nationally.

Market & Audience Behavior Patterns

Seasonal Stacking

Gulkand peaks Mar–Jun (summer cooling). Lozenges peak Oct–Feb (winter throat). Malam is year-round but spikes in monsoon. Adulsa aligns with cough season. The category is inherently seasonal — campaigns should be, too.

Language as Trust Signal

Ayurvedic categories perform 2–3× better when creative is in the viewer's native script — not dubbed, but authored. Marathi in MH, Kannada in KA, Malayalam in Kerala, Gujarati in Gujarat.

Trust > Discount

Unlike D2C beauty/FMCG, Ayurveda buyers respond to heritage, safety, and endorsement — not price drops. Founder-led content, vaidya/doctor quotes, and product-source stories outperform offer-led creatives.

Section 07Investment Framework

Budget Strategy

A recommendation-led framework built around Meta's delivery mechanics — learning-phase stability, auction competitiveness, and reach efficiency.

A. Recommended Daily Budget Range

₹6,000 — ₹12,000 / day across the active portfolio during the expansion phase.

This range is calibrated for:

  • At least 50 optimization events per week per ad set (Meta's learning-phase exit threshold).
  • Parallel activity across 4–6 state clusters without under-delivering in any.
  • Frequency in the 2.5–3.5 range — enough for message recall without fatigue.
  • Buffer for A/B creative testing in every new state entry.

D. Budget-to-Outcome Logic

  • Reach: Budget × region size determines achievable unique reach. Under-budgeted campaigns oscillate — they never stabilize.
  • Frequency: Reach without frequency = wasted spend. Sustained budget for 3+ weeks is what drives recall.
  • Learning Phase: Ad sets under ₹800/day rarely exit learning — Meta cannot optimize on thin signal volume.
  • Cost Efficiency: Splitting budget across too many ad sets is the single biggest efficiency killer. Consolidation > proliferation.
  • Rule of thumb: Fewer, fuller ad sets outperform many starved ones.

Section 07 (cont.)Phased Investment Plan

Phased Investment Plan

Three phases. Three distinct outcomes. Each phase's budget profile earns the right to the next.

Phase 1 — Seed

Month 1 • ~40% of monthly envelope

  • Focus: Kerala + Gujarat entry; keep MH/KA/Goa on maintenance.
  • Objective: Reach + Thru-Play in new states; validate creative-language fit.
  • KPI: CPM, Video Thru-Play %, Saved/Shared content.
  • Rule: Lock creative for 14 days — don't kill before learning.

Phase 2 — Deepen

Month 2 • ~60% envelope uplift

  • Focus: Activate CG + MP using Malam + Adulsa (warm categories).
  • Objective: Layer retargeting + WhatsApp conversion across all 4 new states.
  • KPI: CTR, Landing Page Views, WhatsApp CTR, Cost per Lead.
  • Rule: Move 20% of budget to retargeting pool from week 3.

Phase 3 — Scale

Month 3 • Full envelope deployment

  • Focus: Double down on top-2 new states; build lookalikes from all warm pools.
  • Objective: Move from Reach → Engagement → Purchase/Lead KPIs.
  • KPI: ROAS, Cost per Purchase, Repeat Rate, CPL < ₹40.
  • Rule: Consolidate ad sets — fewer, better-funded groups.

E. Strategic Recommendation

To deliver meaningful expansion outcomes — not just impressions — Amrut Pharma should plan an investment envelope sufficient to run 6–8 well-funded ad sets per state across 6–8 states, with a dedicated always-on retargeting + WhatsApp conversion pool. Budget should be sized to give every ad set a minimum 14-day learning window before optimization decisions are made. Under-investment — spreading spend thin across too many geographies and creatives — is the primary reason Ayurveda brands plateau at ₹1 Cr ARR. Concentrated, patient capital deployment is how brands break through.

WhatsApp Marketing

  • Click-to-WA CTAs on every ad — not just lead-gen campaigns.
  • Always-on WA Business catalog with full Amrut product range.
  • Seasonal broadcast bursts (Gulkand summer, Lozenges winter).
  • WA funnel as cheapest channel for Ayurveda D2C — typical CPL ₹25–45.

Influencer Strategy

  • Tier 1: Ayurveda doctors & vaidyas (trust / authority).
  • Tier 2: Regional home-remedy creators — Marathi, Malayalam, Gujarati, Hindi-belt.
  • Tier 3: Mom-influencers & wellness micro-creators (5k–50k).
  • Barter + paid hybrid; focus on Reels + Carousels, not static posts.

Product-Specific Campaigns

  • Gulkand: Summer ritual, rose-harvest storytelling, gifting during weddings.
  • Adulsa: Monsoon cough prevention, child-safe positioning.
  • Lozenges: Winter + travel/allergy triggers, office desk positioning.
  • Malam: Year-round pain-relief, post-workout & elderly caregiver use cases.

Regional Creative Playbook

  • One master script → adapted across 4–5 regional languages (not auto-translated; authored).
  • State-specific cultural anchors — e.g., Gujarati festivals for Gulkand, Onam for Kerala, Ganesh Chaturthi for MH.
  • Voice-over by native speakers. Talent from the region — not pan-India models.
  • Formats: Reels (9:16), Carousels (1:1), Story-sized ads (9:16), Collection ads for product range.

Retention & Community

  • Monthly "Amrut Ayurveda Almanac" — content asset across WA, email, social.
  • Reorder reminder flows on WhatsApp (30/60/90-day triggers).
  • User-generated recipe / use-case contests for Gulkand & Adulsa.
  • Dedicated dealer/distributor WA broadcast group for B2B-side growth.

Way Forward — 90-Day Plan

A month-by-month rollout calendar. Every action has an owner, an output, and a measurement window.

Month 1

Foundation & Seeding

• Campaign audit + restructure: kill overlap, consolidate ad sets, clean "Unknown" placements.
• Launch Kerala (Malayalam) + Gujarat (Gujarati) Malam & Gulkand awareness campaigns.

Month 2

Deepening & Funnel Activation

• Activate Chhattisgarh & MP campaigns — Malam lead, Adulsa + Lozenges layered.
• Deploy Stage 2 & 3 retargeting (Video viewers → Landing page → WhatsApp).
• Initiate influencer seeding — Tier 2 & 3 creators in all 7 states.
• Launch product-specific campaign clusters (Gulkand summer push in all core + new).
• Weekly optimization reviews; bi-weekly creative refresh for fatigued ad sets.

Month 3

Scale & Lock-In

• Identify top-2 new states by CTR + CPLP View + WhatsApp CPL — concentrate 60% of new-state budget there.
• Shift from Reach to Conversion / Lead objectives on warm audiences.
• Deploy lookalike audiences built from retargeting pools.
• Integrate Quick-commerce placement (Blinkit / Zepto / Swiggy Instamart) tie-ups in MH, KA, Guj.
• Monthly performance review + handoff brief for Q2 roadmap.

Scaling Principles

  • Never scale a campaign that hasn't exited learning phase.
  • Increase budget by max 20% every 3–4 days on winners.
  • Kill only after 14 days or ₹5,000 spend — whichever comes first.
  • Duplicate winners into new geographies before increasing original ad set budget.

Optimization Cadence

  • Daily: budget pacing, frequency checks, anomaly scan.
  • Weekly: creative performance review, audience overlap audit, CPL trend line.
  • Bi-weekly: creative refresh, audience lookalike regeneration.
  • Monthly: full P&L review, expansion/kill decisions, next-month brief sign-off.
